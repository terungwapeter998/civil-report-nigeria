Overview

Abiodun Essiet (born 3 January 1986) is a Nigerian nurse, gender rights activist, community development professional, and politician. She serves as the Senior Special Assistant to the President of Nigeria, Bola Ahmed Tinubu, on Community Engagement, North Central.

Recognized for strengthening grassroots engagement across North-Central Nigeria and advancing the Renewed Hope agenda.

Early Life and Education

Born in Oyo Town and raised in Ibadan, she attended Ifeoluwa Nursery and Primary School, Queens School Ibadan, and United Missionary Comprehensive College where she completed secondary education in 2002.

She earned a Bachelor of Science in Nursing from LAUTECH, a Master’s in Public Health from the University of Ibadan, and later obtained a PhD in International Development from the University of International Business and Economics, Beijing in 2025.

Career

Her career spans public health, gender advocacy, and community development. Through the Abiodun Essiet Initiative for Girls, she works against human trafficking and gender-based violence in collaboration with NAPTIP.

Political Career

She transitioned into politics in 2016. She contested in AMAC elections and later served as SSA on ICT, Donor Agencies and Civil Societies before her appointment by President Tinubu in 2024.

She established the Citizens’ Assembly for the North-Central Region to improve governance participation.
